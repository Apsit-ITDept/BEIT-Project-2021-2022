Cabbage

Soil and Climate

1. For early crop light soils are best whereas for late crop heavy soils are preferred.
2. The optimum soil pH is 6.0 to 6.5.
3. It requires cool and moist climate.

Time of Sowing

In lower hills of Meghalaya, cabbage is grown mainly in winter season (October-January), while in higher hills it is grown in both rainy and winter seasons. In mid hills, cabbage can be grown almost throughout the year.

Manure and Fertilizers

1. It is recommended that poultry manure be applied at 6 t/ha for increased nutrient availability, growth and head yield of cabbage as well as enhancing sustainable cultivation of cabbage on commercial basis.
2. The use of poultry manure would replace the application of large quantity of NPK 15-15-15 fertilizer

Pesticides

1. Insecticides containing Bacillus thuringiensis are used to control armyworms, cutworms, diamondback moths, imported cabbage worms and loopers on cabbage.
2. Treat armyworms when they first hatch and repeat every three days to two weeks as new armyworms hatch.
