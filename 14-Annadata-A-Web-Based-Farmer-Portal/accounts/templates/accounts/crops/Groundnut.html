Groundnut

Soil and Climate

1. Peanuts grow best in loose, well-drained soil rich in organic matter. A sandy-loamy soil is best. Double-dig clay soil and add gypsum and aged compost.
2. The soil must be loose so that the pegs can penetrate and grow.
3. Optimum mean daily temperature to grow is 30°C and growth ceases at 15°C. For rapid emergence, soil temperature above 21°C is needed.

Time of Sowing

Kharif-groundnut- June to July subject to onset of monsoon, Rabi groundnut-November and Summer groundnut-February-March.

Manure and Fertilizers

Use of either farmyard manure (FYM) and vermicompost along with other organic amendments like neem seed cake; and bio- fertilizers and bio-pesticides etc., in rainfed groundnut, found advantageous for sustainable crop production.

Pesticides

1. Spray dimethoate 30EC @ 650ml/ha 2. Monochrotophos 36SL @ 600ml/ha in 600 liter water.
