Brinjal

Soil and Climate

1. It can be grown in all types of soils, but well drained fertile, rich in organic matter silt or clay loam soils are ideal.
2. The optimum pH is between 5.5-6.6, although it can tolerate a little soil acidity. Sandy loam soil is best for early crop and clay loam soil for higher yield.
3. It is a warm season crop and requires a relatively long growing season with plenty of sunshine and moderate day temperature of 21 0 -27 0 C.
4. Extreme temperature (below 10 0 C and above 30 0 C ) adversely affects fruit setting. Warm and humid climate favours luxurious growth and cool climate restricts the growth.

Time of Sowing

brinjal can be grown round the year, the main sowing season being during July to August. For a second crop of brinjal, seeds are sown from March to April. Seedlings are grown on raised nursery bed of 15 cm height.

Manure and Fertilizers

1. The fertilizer dose depends upon the fertility of soil and amount of organic manure applied to the crop.
2. For a good yield, 15-20 tonnes of well-decomposed FYM is incorporated into the soil.
3. Generally, application of 150 kg N, 100 kg P2 O5 and 50 kg K2 O is recommended for optimum yield.

Pesticides

1. Spraying of Carbaryl (0.1%) or Malathion (0.05%) controls the pest effectively.
2. Red Spider mite (Tetracycline neocaledonicus, Tetranychus cinnabarinus,Paratetranychus indicus): The mite is a pest of brinjal. ... Acaricides like Dicofol (0.05%) and Wettable Sulphur (0.3%) gives effective control of mites.
