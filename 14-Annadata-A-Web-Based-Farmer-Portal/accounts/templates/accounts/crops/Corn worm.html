Earhead feeders (Corn worm/Earworm: Helicoverpa armigera)

Symptoms of Damage

Larva feeds on silk and developing grains.

Identification of Pest

1. Eggs - Spherical in shape and creamy white in colour, laid singly
2. Larva - Shows colour variation from greenish to brown.
3. It has dark brown grey lines on the body with lateral white lines
4. Pupa - Brown in colour, occurs in soil, leaf, pod and crop debris

Adult

1. Light pale brownish yellow stout moth.
2. Forewings are olive green to pale brown with a dark brown circular spot in the centre.
3. Hind wings are pale smoky white with a broad blackish outer margin.

Remedy

• Set up of light traps
• Set up sex pheromone traps at 12/ha
• Two applications of NPV at 10 days interval at 1.5 X1012 POB along with Crude sugar 2.5 kg + cotton seed kernel powder 250 g on the ear heads
Apply any one of the following on 3 rd and 18 th day after panicle emergence :
• Carbaryl 10 D @ 25 kg/ha
• Malathion 5 D @ 25 kg/ha
• Phosalone 4 D @ 25 kg/ha
